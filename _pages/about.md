My name is Raphaël Tournier, I'm 28 years old and i'm second year PhD student at Université Grenoble Alpes (UGA), working on Life and Earth sciences. 

In my work, I use numerical modeling tools to reconstruct the coevolution of the various components of the Earth system.

## PhD
My PhD is entitled "Hominin dispersal in the changing landscapes of the East African Rift".

The goal is to explore various questions related to the hominin clade (e.g., its geographic range, evolutionary timing, and dispersal) using a deductive approach based on numerical reconstructions.
This type of tool helps overcome the limitation posed by incomplete field data by continuously reconstructing landscapes across time and space.
Modeling the Earth system involves many fields, including: 
1. Dynamic topography with Laurent Husson
2. Tectonics with Nicolas Bellahsen 
3. Climate modelling (IPSL-CM5A2) with Pierre Sepulchre
4. Physiography ( gospl LEM) with Tristan Salles
5. Vegetation (Orchidee) with Pierre Sepulchre
6. Life dynamics (PaleoSDM) with Corentin Gibert

My PhD director is Laurent Husson, and my surpervisor are Jean-Renaud Boisserie and Sandrine Prat.
My PhD is funded by a MITI 80 PRIME CNRS project.
